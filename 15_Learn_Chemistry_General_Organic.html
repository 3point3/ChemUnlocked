General Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the chemistry of carbon compounds. This unit introduces the bonding patterns, hydrocarbon families, functional groups, and reaction ideas that help you recognize organic molecules without memorizing every structure.

15.1 Why Carbon Is Special

Carbon has four valence electrons and usually forms four covalent bonds. It can bond to H, O, N, halogens, and other carbon atoms, creating chains, rings, branches, and multiple bonds.

▸

Core pattern: neutral carbon usually has 4 bonds, neutral nitrogen usually has 3 bonds and 1 lone pair, neutral oxygen usually has 2 bonds and 2 lone pairs, and hydrogen forms 1 bond.

15.5 Isomers

Isomers have the same molecular formula but different structures. Different structures can create very different properties, even when the atom counts match.

▸

Example: C4H10 can be drawn as a straight chain (butane) or as a branched chain (2-methylpropane). Same formula, different connectivity.

Ex 1 Identifying a Functional Group

Given: CH3CH2OH.  Find: the functional group and compound family.

Look for heteroatoms

The molecule contains oxygen in an -OH group.

Match the pattern

An -OH group attached to carbon identifies an alcohol.

Name the simple molecule

Two carbons gives the root eth-. The alcohol ending gives ethanol.

Ex 2 Writing a Combustion Reaction

Given: methane burns completely.  Find: the balanced equation.

Write the skeleton reaction

CH4 + O2 → CO2 + H2O

Balance C and H first

One carbon gives 1 CO2. Four hydrogens give 2 H2O.

Balance oxygen last

The products contain 4 oxygen atoms total, so the reactants need 2 O2.

Final equation

CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O
