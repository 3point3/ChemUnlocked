Why Practice Tests Beat Rereading Every Time

Practice tests work because they force you to pull information out of memory instead of laying eyes on it one more time. That is much closer to what your brain has to do on a real quiz or exam.

⊕ ChemUnlocked Study Skills ◷ 7 min read ✦ Research-backed

A lot of students reread their notes because it feels productive. The material looks familiar. It seems smoother. The problem is that familiarity is not the same as being able to recall and use the material when nobody is holding the answer sheet open for you.

Practice tests are different because they force retrieval. You have to pull the information out of memory, organize it, and use it. That is much closer to what happens during an actual test.

What Retrieval Means

Retrieval means bringing information back without looking at it first. That might mean answering a practice question, solving a problem with your notes closed, or explaining an idea out loud from memory.

That is why retrieval is so powerful. It does not just reveal whether you know something. It strengthens the memory itself. Rereading lets you recognize information when it is in front of you. Retrieval makes you rebuild it when it is not.

◈ Why This Helps
In one well-known study, students who practiced pulling information out of memory remembered far more a week later than students who mostly restudied. This effect is strong enough that researchers see it over and over.

Why Rereading Feels Better Than It Is

✗ Low Utility
  • Re-reading notes or textbook
  • Highlighting or underlining
  • Summarizing (passively)
  • Keyword mnemonics
  • Imagery for text learning
✓ High Utility
  • Practice testing (retrieval)
  • Distributed practice (spaced)
  • Interleaved practice
  • Elaborative interrogation
  • Self-explanation

Students often default to rereading, highlighting, and passive review because those strategies feel smooth. You are looking at the material, it seems clear, and nothing feels especially hard. The problem is that easy studying is not always effective studying.

The strategies on the right side of that chart usually feel harder because they demand more thinking. That is not a flaw. That extra effort is often exactly why the learning lasts.

Why Getting It Wrong Is Part of the Process

One reason students avoid practice tests is that they hate getting answers wrong while they study. Fair enough. But being wrong during practice is not failure. It is information.

When you try to retrieve an answer and miss it, the gap becomes easier to see. Then, when you check the correct answer or read the worked solution, your brain has something specific to attach that correction to. The mistake becomes part of the learning instead of just an embarrassment you try to avoid.

How to Use This

Do Not Skip the Try

When you are not sure how to answer a practice question, try anyway. Write something. Pick an approach. Then check your work and read the solution carefully. Even if you land on the right answer, make sure your reasoning was actually sound. The attempt matters.

It Works Even Better If You Come Back Later

Practice tests get even better when you return to the material more than once over time. That is spaced practice. Instead of doing all your review in one sitting, you revisit the same ideas across several days.

Practical Application

A Simple Return Schedule

Try the material the day you learn it. Come back a few days later. Come back again about a week after that. Each time, retrieve before you look at notes. You do not need a perfect schedule. You do need to return after some forgetting has happened.

◈ Why This Helps
Spacing works. Students remember more when they come back to material over time instead of cramming it all into one session. We have known that for a long time, even if cramming is still popular.

How ChemUnlocked Practice Helps

The ChemUnlocked practice sets are built around retrieval. You get a small set of problems at a time, and the questions change. That matters because it stops you from leaning too hard on recognition or memory of a previous answer.

The sets also include different kinds of thinking: calculations, conceptual questions, and multi-step problems. That mix helps you practice choosing an approach instead of just repeating one familiar pattern.

The worked solutions are there for a reason. The goal is not just to see whether you got it right. The goal is to compare your thinking to a correct approach and improve from that comparison.

The Honest Bottom Line

The students who grow the most in chemistry are usually not the ones doing the most passive review. They are the ones willing to attempt, check, correct, and try again. That takes more effort, but it is a far better use of study time.

Start with one unit. Try a practice set before you feel completely ready. Get some wrong if that is how it goes. Read the solutions carefully. Then come back later and do it again. That cycle of retrieve, check, and return is one of the best study habits you can build.

▸

If a study session feels easy because you are mostly rereading and recognizing, be careful. That can create confidence without much real recall. Better studying usually includes some struggle, because you are asking your brain to bring the material back and use it.
